📌 Project Overview
This project conducts an Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) analysis of American Express during its transition to EMV chip card technology in the U.S. market. The analysis covers the competitive risks associated with this technology adoption, including threats from Visa and Mastercard's faster deployment, the rise of Card-Not-Present (CNP) fraud, and the opportunity presented by the growing contactless and mobile payment market.

📊 SWOT Analysis
The SWOT analysis identifies internal and external factors affecting Amex during the EMV transition:

Strengths — Closed-loop network control, global EMV experience, Fraud Liability Shift (FLS) policy
Weaknesses — Higher merchant fees, CNP fraud vulnerability, merchant terminal delays
Opportunities — Counterfeit fraud reduction, Expresspay contactless platform, premium cardholder loyalty
Threats — Visa/Mastercard acceptance advantage, CNP fraud escalation, competitive mobile wallet space
